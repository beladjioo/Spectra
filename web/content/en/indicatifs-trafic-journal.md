# Callsigns, operating and the logbook

**The callsign** is your radio identity, unique worldwide. It reads in two parts: a **prefix** that designates the country (allocated by the ITU — French prefixes start with F, for instance) followed by a **suffix** specific to the station. Spoken aloud, you spell it with the [[code-q-phonetique-rst|phonetic alphabet]] to avoid any confusion.

**Golden rule**: you must **identify yourself** regularly (at the start and end of a contact, and periodically during it) by giving your callsign. Transmitting without identifying is forbidden.

**The flow of a contact (QSO)**:
- **General call**: "CQ CQ CQ de [your callsign]" to solicit a contact.
- **Reply**: give your callsign, exchange a **report** (see [[code-q-phonetique-rst|RST]]), first name, location, equipment.
- **Closing**: say goodbye and each station re-identifies.

Additional prefixes/suffixes signal a situation: portable station, mobile, or operating in another country under the **CEPT** agreement (see [[reglementation]]).

**The logbook** records contacts (date, UTC time, frequency, mode, the other station's callsign, reports). Beyond any legal obligation, it's a precious tool for tracking [[propagation]] and your own performance.
